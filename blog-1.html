The Law of Conservation of Mass

The law of conservation of mass states that the mass of a system is constant. Mass can be neither created nor destroyed, but it can be converted into energy.

Law of Conservation of Energy

The principles of conservation of energy state that the total energy of a system is constant. Energy can be neither created nor destroyed, but it can be converted from one form to another.

Law of Convservation of Momentum

The principle of conservation of momentum states that the total momentum of a system is constant. Momentum can be neither created nor destroyed, but it can be transferred from one object to another.

The Principle of Least Action

The principle of least action states that the path of a particle is the path of least action. This principle is based on the principle of least time, which states that the path of a light ray is the path of least time.

The Principle of Least Time

The principle of least time states that the path of a light ray is the path of least time. This principle is based on the principle of least action, which states that the path of a particle is the path of least action.

As you can see, there is a reciprocal interplay between the principle of least action and the principle of least time. A particle acts as a wave and a wave acts as a particle.
