Eliminative materialism

The eliminative materialist perspective is that the human mind is not real because it is a construct of the brain. The mind is encapsulated in the active dynamic core network of the brain and the mind as a physical entity does not exist outside of this active dynamic core network.

Pluralism

The eliminative perspective is that there are many minds. There are many minds because there are many qualia, many ways of experiencing the world. The conscious experience of the average human feels like they have "one" concrete mind because our human biology produces only one qualia. This is a functional illusion that our minds are one.

Active questions: Do we experience multiple qualia in waking conscious life?

Non-Reductionism

The belief that mental states are not reducible to physical states.
